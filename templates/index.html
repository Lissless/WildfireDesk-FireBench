About Sage

Sage’s mission is to support wildfire recovery by helping people take informed action and work together with their communities to rebuild and move forward.

It is a non-partisan, non-commercial AI assistant focused on practical guidance and community-driven solutions.

How Sage Can Help

Sage is a civically minded chatbot that helps you understand your options and take practical steps after a wildfire. It supports both individual decisions and community efforts so you can move forward with clarity.

  • Understand your options for FEMA, insurance, and recovery
  • Plan next steps for housing, rebuilding, and navigating systems
  • Organize with neighbors and coordinate community efforts
  • Create materials like meeting agendas, outreach messages, and action plans

Privacy

Your privacy matters. Sage is designed to support you without storing personal information.

  • We do not store your personal data or chat history.
  • Your messages are only used to generate responses during this session.
  • Conversations are not saved, shared, or used for training.
If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

Welcome to Sage

Sage is a civically minded chatbot that helps you think through wildfire recovery, rebuilding, insurance, housing, and community action.

How to use Sage

  1. Start with a suggested question, or type something on your mind.
  2. Answer any follow-up questions so Sage understands your situation.
  3. Use the response to plan next steps, compare options, or draft materials.
Try: “Help me draft an outreach message for a community meeting.”

Response mode

Response mode controls what kind of information Sage uses when answering.

How to choose

  • Expert-informed: uses curated research and expert resources to provide more grounded, evidence-based guidance.
  • General response: generates flexible, general-purpose answers that are useful for brainstorming, drafting, or exploring ideas.

Local news context

Local news helps Sage tailor answers to what’s happening in your area.

How to use it

  1. Turn on “Include local news context.”
  2. Select a state.
  3. Choose a community, or leave it as “All communities.”
